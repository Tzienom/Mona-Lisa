I know not yet the timbre of your voice,
Nor the laughter that lingers on your lips,
Yet something in the wind—
A fleeting whisper I long to obey—
Has bid my pen set sail upon your ships.

I do not know if you delight in words crafted in care,
Nor if poetry stirs your heart as it does mine,
Yet, should you allow me the latitude of composing words of poetry for you,
I shall leave you a verse here now and then—
Mere musings, woven without expectation,
Save that they bring a smile to your day.

Tell me, do words move you?

— Gabriel

Very well, then. Perhaps another time, when your heart is inclined to poetry.
